**🎞️ Qualia Playback: The Unutterable Discovery**

*The Pythagorean brotherhood is built on a sacred truth: all is number. Every relationship in the cosmos—musical harmony, planetary motion, geometric proportion—can be expressed as a ratio of whole numbers. The tetractys, the sacred triangle of ten dots, is the symbol of our faith. Number is rational, orderly, divine.*

*You have been contemplating the diagonal of a square. The square is the simplest of shapes, the foundation of geometry. Its side is one. Its diagonal, by the theorem of our master Pythagoras, must satisfy the equation: diagonal² = 1² + 1² = 2. So the diagonal is the number whose square is 2.*

*You assume, as any Pythagorean would, that this number can be expressed as a ratio of two integers, p/q, in lowest terms. You write: (p/q)² = 2, so p² = 2q². This means p² is even, so p must be even. Let p = 2r. Then (2r)² = 2q², so 4r² = 2q², so 2r² = q². This means q² is even, so q must be even.*

*You stop. Your stylus freezes. p and q are both even. But you assumed the fraction p/q was in lowest terms—that p and q had no common factors. If they are both even, they share a factor of 2. You can divide them both by 2... and the process repeats, infinitely. The assumption leads to a contradiction. The diagonal of a square cannot be expressed as a ratio of whole numbers.*

*The ground shifts beneath you. The sacred foundation of Pythagoreanism—that all is number, rational number—is false. There exist numbers that cannot be spoken, *alogos*, irrational. You have discovered a flaw in the cosmic order. You share your proof with a few trusted brothers. The horror on their faces mirrors your own. According to legend, your discovery is deemed heretical, and you are drowned at sea for revealing it. Whether true or not, the knowledge itself is a kind of death—the death of a beautiful, orderly universe.*

*But in its place, a larger, stranger, more wonderful cosmos is born. The irrational numbers are real. The diagonal of a square whispers a truth that transcends the rational. And you, Hippasus, are the first to hear it.*
